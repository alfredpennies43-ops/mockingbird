Field notes from
the velvet garden

Read alongside the broadcast. Each issue opens with one long letter from a contributor in the field.

Color is a slow language. Lucidbloom is built for readers who notice — who walk back to look at the same purple twice, who keep a quiet record of the way light folds at the end of an afternoon.

We started this almanac because the radio felt thinner each year. The slow shows had vanished from the public dial. The strange ones too. We wanted a place to gather what we still believed in: long-form mixes, hand-drawn diagrams, a few good recipes for hibiscus tea, a year-long correspondence between a herbalist in Lisbon and a fungal artist on Vashon Island.

Volume 04 is the equinox edition. We weave together three voices — a colorist, a gardener, and a sound healer — and a single guiding question: what blooms inside us when nothing else is asked of the day?

The trick was never to find a brighter color, but to stay in the violet long enough for the eyes to admit the dark was full of light all along.

M
Mira Pesoa Editor, Vol. 04 · Lisbon
